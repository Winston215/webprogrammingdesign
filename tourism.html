Raja Ampat

West Papua, Indonesia

Known as the "Four Kings," Raja Ampat is an archipelago comprising over 1,500 small islands, cays, and shoals. It's home to the world's richest marine biodiversity, with over 1,300 fish species and 600 coral species.

Borobudur Temple

Central Java, Indonesia

The world's largest Buddhist temple, built in the 9th century. This UNESCO World Heritage site features 2,672 relief panels and 504 Buddha statues, showcasing the story of Buddha's life and teachings.

Bali

Bali, Indonesia

Known as the "Island of the Gods," Bali offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and modern amenities. From pristine beaches to lush rice terraces and sacred temples.

Komodo National Park

East Nusa Tenggara, Indonesia

Home to the world's largest living lizard, the Komodo dragon. This UNESCO World Heritage site offers unique wildlife encounters, stunning landscapes, and world-class diving spots.

Mount Bromo

East Java, Indonesia

An active volcano in East Java, famous for its dramatic landscape and stunning sunrise views. The surrounding Sea of Sand and the Tengger caldera create a surreal, otherworldly atmosphere.
