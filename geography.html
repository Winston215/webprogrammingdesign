Remarkable Geographical Features

Raja Ampat Islands

Located off the coast of West Papua, Raja Ampat is an archipelago comprising over 1,500 small islands, cays, and shoals surrounding four main islands. It is globally recognized for its unparalleled marine biodiversity, often referred to as the 'Amazon of the seas'. Its stunning karst landscapes rising from the clear blue waters create a truly breathtaking scenery.

Mount Bromo

An active volcano in East Java, Mount Bromo is one of Indonesia's most iconic landmarks. Surrounded by the Sea of Sand, a vast volcanic desert, the view of the sunrise over Mount Bromo with the surrounding peaks and caldera is a popular tourist attraction. The landscape is dramatic and otherworldly, a testament to Indonesia's volcanic activity.

Lake Toba

Situated in North Sumatra, Lake Toba is the world's largest volcanic lake, formed after a supervolcanic eruption tens of thousands of years ago. It's not just a lake; it has an island in the middle, Samosir Island, which is almost the size of Singapore. The lake is a major tourist destination, offering stunning views, a cool climate, and insights into the unique Batak culture.
